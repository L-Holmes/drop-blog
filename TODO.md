* Verifiable real-world business presence
  * Show a real company name, address, phone number, author identity, and About page
  * Example: “HealthStride Media Ltd — Manchester, UK” with author bio for “Best Shoes for Plantar Fasciitis”

* Independent third-party reviews and proof
  * Embed ratings or references from trusted external platforms
  * Example: “Rated 4.8 on Trustpilot” linked beside “Best Productivity Apps for ADHD”

* Detailed testing methodology
  * Explain exactly how products were tested and compared
  * Example: “We tested 23 standing desks over 6 weeks for stability, noise, and comfort”

* Quantified evidence and specifics
  * Use measurable details instead of vague praise
  * Example: “Battery lasted 14h 22m during continuous Zoom calls”

* Real author expertise
  * Clearly show who wrote the content and why they’re qualified
  * Example: “Written by a licensed physiotherapist with 12 years in gait analysis”

* Authentic custom photography
  * Use your own photos of products, people, or testing setups
  * Example: Original photos of someone actually wearing and testing the shoes reviewed

* Fast, polished technical UX
  * Ensure pages load quickly and work flawlessly on mobile
  * Example: “Best Meditation Apps” article loads instantly with no broken filters or lag

* Clear editorial standards
  * Publicly explain how rankings, affiliate links, and recommendations work
  * Example: “Products are ranked independently; commissions do not affect placement”

* Transparent affiliate disclosure
  * State clearly when links earn commissions
  * Example: “We may earn a commission if you buy through links in this article”

* Context-rich testimonials or user experiences
  * Include detailed stories with real context and outcomes
  * Example: “After switching to Shoe X, my knee pain reduced within 3 weeks of daily walking”

* Scannable trust formatting
  * Make key proof easy to skim with bolded highlights and summaries
  * Example: “Best battery life” badge beside the top productivity app pick

* Real product usage demonstrations
  * Show products in realistic use cases instead of isolated promo shots
  * Example: Photos of running shoes after outdoor testing on pavement and trails

* Upfront transparency on rankings
  * Explain why products are ranked where they are
  * Example: “Ranked #1 because it had the best arch support and durability score”

* Human-centered design
  * Make the site feel genuinely operated by real people
  * Example: Team page with real writers, editors, and testers instead of faceless branding

* Strong About and Contact pages
  * Make it obvious who runs the site and how to reach them
  * Example: Dedicated editorial office contact page with named staff

* Real-world operational proof
  * Show behind-the-scenes evidence of your process
  * Example: Video walkthrough of your desk-testing process for ergonomic office gear

* Consistent branding and localization
  * Match tone, imagery, spelling, and audience expectations naturally
  * Example: UK-focused health blog using UK spelling, pricing, and healthcare references

* Minimal friction before value delivery
  * Let users read content before asking for signup or email
  * Example: Full “Best Sleep Apps” rankings visible without registration

* Clear comparison tables
  * Make comparisons objective and easy to evaluate quickly
  * Example: Table comparing cushioning, arch support, price, and durability of shoes

* Real external citations and references
  * Link to studies, expert sources, and official data
  * Example: Referencing peer-reviewed research on foot support and joint pain

* Frequently updated content
  * Keep recommendations current and visibly maintained
  * Example: “Updated June 2026 after retesting 8 new models”

* Unique imagery and assets
  * Use visuals not found on dozens of other websites
  * Example: Your own screenshots of productivity apps instead of App Store marketing images

* Honest pros and cons
  * Include downsides instead of pretending every product is perfect
  * Example: “Excellent cushioning, but too heavy for speed workouts”

* Real user intent alignment
  * Prioritize helping the reader solve a problem instead of maximizing clicks
  * Example: Recommending cheaper shoes when they genuinely outperform premium brands

* Stable, uncluttered interface
  * Avoid excessive ads, popups, autoplay videos, and distractions
  * Example: Simple reading experience for “Best Vitamins for Energy” with clean navigation

* Clear trust signals near buying decisions
  * Place credibility indicators where users hesitate most
  * Example: Expert testing badge beside affiliate buttons

* Informational imagery only
  * Every image should help decision-making or understanding
  * Example: Side-by-side sole wear comparison between two walking shoes

* Real-world specificity
  * Include details that prove firsthand experience
  * Example: “The heel padding started compressing after roughly 120km of use”

* Visible content ownership
  * Show who edits, reviews, and maintains the site
  * Example: “Reviewed by Dr. Sarah Ahmed, DPM” under every health-related article
