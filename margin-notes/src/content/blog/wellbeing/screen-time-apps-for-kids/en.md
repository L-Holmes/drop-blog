Quick version: start with the free controls already on the phone. If you need more, a dedicated app is worth it. Everything below comes from months of real use — no affiliate links, nobody paid us.

## How we test, vs how most "best app" lists work

| | 💚 Margin Notes | Most "top 10" lists |
|---|---|---|
| Who writes it | ✓ Parents using the apps daily | Sites chasing commission |
| Actually tested? | ✓ Yes, months, our own kids | ✗ Often not at all |
| Affiliate links | ✓ None | ✗ On every button |
| Tell you the downsides | ✓ Always | ✗ Rarely |

## The results

- Best free start: the phone's built-in controls. Daily limits, bedtime, app approvals, nothing to install.
- Best for younger kids: a dedicated app with content filtering. Worth its monthly fee.
- Best for older kids: a lighter time-limit app that doesn't feel like spying.
- Skip: anything that hides itself from your child. It causes more rows, not fewer.

One honest note: prices and features change often, so check before you commit. And no app replaces the conversation. The limit is the easy bit; the why is what sticks.
